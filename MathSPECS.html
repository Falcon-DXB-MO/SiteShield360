1. Introduction

The AI-CRV engine fuses risk indicators from six independent modules into a single composite risk score \(S_{\text{final}} \in [0,100]\). Each module first normalizes raw sensor or operational data into a raw risk score \(S_i^{\text{raw}} \in [0,100]\). These are then smoothed via time‑aware exponential moving averages, fused using confidence‑weighted averaging, and finally subjected to an asymmetric multi‑level override and composite smoothing. The result is a robust, explainable, and temporally stable risk metric.

📐 Pipeline Steps (logical order, matching code comments):
1. Normalization → 2. Raw Confidence → 3. Per‑Module EMA Smoothing → 4. Confidence‑Weighted Fusion →
5. Multi‑Level Override → 6. Composite EMA Smoothing → 7. Explainability (Contributions).

2. Notation

\[ \begin{array}{ll} i & \text{Module index: threat, ops, rigFloor, vehicle, radioactive, explosive} \\ S_i^{\text{raw}}(t) & \text{Raw normalized risk score of module }i\text{ at time }t \\ S_i^{\text{smooth}}(t) & \text{Smoothed risk score after per‑module EMA} \\ S_{\text{raw}}(t) & \text{Confidence‑weighted fusion result (before override)} \\ S_{\text{override}}(t) & \text{Score after multi‑level override} \\ S_{\text{instant}}(t) & \text{Instantaneous composite (override result)} \\ S_{\text{final}}(t) & \text{Final composite after temporal smoothing} \\ c_i^{\text{raw}}(t) & \text{Raw confidence of module }i\;(0\text{ to }1) \\ c_i^{\text{eff}}(t) & \text{Effective confidence for weighting }=\max(c_i^{\text{raw}},\text{softFloor}) \\ w_i & \text{Static weight assigned to module }i,\;\sum w_i = 1 \\ \alpha_i(t) & \text{Dynamic EMA smoothing factor for module }i \\ \tau_i & \text{Time constant for module }i\text{ (ms)} \\ \Delta t & \text{Time elapsed since last update }= t - t_{\text{prev}} \end{array} \]

All scores and confidences are clamped to their respective ranges after every operation.

3. Layer 1 – Module‑Specific Normalization

Each module transforms its input data into a raw risk score \(S_i^{\text{raw}} \in [0,100]\). Default behaviours and fallback values are defined for missing or invalid inputs.

3.1 Threat Module

Inputs: distance \(d\) [km], speed \(v\) [m/s], risk zone \(z \in \{\text{LOW},\text{MEDIUM},\text{HIGH}\}\), maximum distance \(D_{\max}\) (default 5 km).

\[ \begin{aligned} d' &= \min(d, D_{\max}) \\ \text{distanceFactor} &= 100 \cdot \frac{D_{\max} - d'}{D_{\max}} \\[4pt] v' &= \min(v, 51) \\ \text{speedFactor} &= 100 \cdot \frac{v'}{51} \\[4pt] \text{zoneFactor} &= \begin{cases} 30 & z = \text{HIGH} \\ 15 & z = \text{MEDIUM} \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \\[4pt] S_{\text{threat}}^{\text{raw}} &= \text{round}\bigl(0.50 \cdot \text{distanceFactor} + 0.30 \cdot \text{speedFactor} + \text{zoneFactor}\bigr) \end{aligned} \]

If no threat is detected, baseline score 5 is returned.

3.2 Operational Hazards (Ops)

Input: list of hazard risk scores \(h_1,\dots,h_n\), each \(h_j\in[0,100]\). Let \(m = \max_j h_j\), \(\mu = \frac{1}{n}\sum h_j\). Blending weights \(\alpha_{\max}=0.60\), \(\alpha_{\text{avg}}=0.40\).

\[ S_{\text{ops}}^{\text{raw}} = \text{round}\bigl(\alpha_{\max} \cdot m + \alpha_{\text{avg}} \cdot \mu\bigr) \]

If no hazards, default \(S=10\). If no active operation, default \(S=5\).

3.3 Rig Floor Safety

Input: active detections with severity mapping: CRITICAL→35, HIGH→25, MEDIUM→15, LOW→8. Sum of mapped scores = \(Q\).

\[ S_{\text{rig}}^{\text{raw}} = \text{round}\bigl(\min(Q, 100)\bigr) \]

If no active detections, default \(S=8\).

3.4 Vehicle Safety

For each moving vehicle, risk score \(r_k\). \(R_{\max} = \max_k r_k\), \(\bar r = \frac{1}{K}\sum r_k\). Blending \(\beta_{\max}=0.60\), \(\beta_{\text{avg}}=0.40\).

\[ S_{\text{vehicle}}^{\text{raw}} = \text{round}\bigl(\beta_{\max} \cdot R_{\max} + \beta_{\text{avg}} \cdot \bar r\bigr) \]

If no moving vehicles, default \(S=5\).

3.5 Radioactive Source

Inputs: location \(L\), cable tension \(T\) [kN], stuck threshold \(T_{\text{stuck}}\) (default 80).
Base score \(B\):

\[ B = \begin{cases}5 & L=\text{SHIELDED}\\50 & L=\text{EXTRACTED}\\70 & L=\text{UNKNOWN}\end{cases} \qquad F_T = 100 \cdot \min\!\left(1,\frac{T}{T_{\text{stuck}}}\right) \] \[ S_{\text{radio}}^{\text{raw}} = \begin{cases} \min(100,\; B + 0.5F_T) & B \ge 50 \\ \min(100,\; B + 0.3F_T) & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \]

Unconditional tension override: if \(F_T \ge 90\), then \(S_{\text{radio}}^{\text{raw}} = \max(S_{\text{radio}}^{\text{raw}}, 85)\).

Default \(S=5\) if module state missing.

3.6 Explosive Device

Inputs: armed flag \(A\), depth \(D\), safe depth \(D_{\text{safe}}\) (default 500 m), sequence valid \(V\), rig tension \(T_{\text{rig}}\), max tension \(T_{\max}=500\) kN.

\[ S_{\text{expl}}^{\text{raw}} = \begin{cases} 100 & V = \text{false} \\ 100 & A = \text{true and } D < D_{\text{safe}} \\ 60 + 30\left(1 - \frac{D}{D_{\text{safe}}}\right) + 0.2\cdot100\cdot\frac{\min(T_{\text{rig}},T_{\max})}{T_{\max}} & A = \text{true and } D \ge D_{\text{safe}} \\ 5 & \text{otherwise (not armed)} \end{cases} \]

4. Layer 2 – Composite Inference Engine

4.1 Step 1: Normalization (already described above)

Produces \(S_i^{\text{raw}}\) for all modules.

4.2 Step 2: Raw Confidence \(c_i^{\text{raw}}\)

Confidence is computed from recency, health, visibility, and model confidence factors:

\[ c_i^{\text{raw}} = \text{clamp}\bigl( f_{\text{recency}} \cdot f_{\text{health}} \cdot f_{\text{visibility}} \cdot f_{\text{model}} ,\; 0,\; 1 \bigr) \]
  • \(f_{\text{recency}} = 0.60\) if last update > 10 s else 1.0
  • \(f_{\text{health}} = 1.0\) (healthy), \(0.70\) (degraded), \(0.20\) (offline)
  • \(f_{\text{visibility}} = 1.0\) (normal), \(0.60\) (low)
  • \(f_{\text{model}} = \text{clamp}(\text{modelConfidence},0,1)\) if provided, else 1.0

Modules with \(c_i^{\text{raw}} < \text{minModuleConfidence}\) (default 0.1) are excluded from fusion.

4.3 Step 3: Per‑Module Temporal Smoothing (EMA)

Let \(\bar S_i(t-1)\) be the previous smoothed value, \(t_{\text{prev}}\) last update timestamp. At current time \(t\) with raw score \(S_i^{\text{raw}}(t)\), compute smoothing factor:

\[ \alpha_i(t) = \begin{cases} 1 - \exp\!\left(-\dfrac{\Delta t}{\tau_i}\right) & \text{if } \tau_i \text{ given and } \Delta t > 0 \\ \alpha_i^{\text{fixed}} & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \quad\text{where }\Delta t = t - t_{\text{prev}}\text{ (ms)} \] \[ S_i^{\text{smooth}}(t) = \alpha_i(t) \cdot S_i^{\text{raw}}(t) + \bigl(1 - \alpha_i(t)\bigr) \cdot \bar S_i(t-1) \]

After updating, store \(\bar S_i(t) = S_i^{\text{smooth}}(t)\) and set \(t_{\text{prev}} = t\).

4.4 Step 4: Confidence‑Weighted Fusion

Active set \(\mathcal{A} = \{ i \mid c_i^{\text{raw}} \ge \text{minModuleConfidence} \}\). Effective confidence (also used for explainability):

\[ c_i^{\text{eff}} = \max\bigl(c_i^{\text{raw}},\; \text{confidenceSoftFloor}\bigr) \quad (\text{default soft floor }0.2) \]

The raw fused score is:

\[ S_{\text{raw}} = \begin{cases} \dfrac{\sum_{i \in \mathcal{A}} S_i^{\text{smooth}} \cdot w_i \cdot c_i^{\text{eff}}}{\sum_{i \in \mathcal{A}} w_i \cdot c_i^{\text{eff}}} & \text{if denominator } \ge 0.01 \\[6pt] \text{average of } \mathbf{S_i^{\text{raw}}} \text{ (module raw scores, not smoothed) for } i \in \mathcal{A} & \text{otherwise (fallback)} \end{cases} \]
Clarification: The fallback uses the module raw scores \(S_i^{\text{raw}}\) (pre‑EMA) to avoid biasing the average with old, possibly outdated smoothed values when confidence is extremely low.

4.5 Step 5: Multi‑Level Critical Override

Hybrid score per module: \(H_i = \max(S_i^{\text{raw}}, S_i^{\text{smooth}})\), and \(S_{\max} = \max_i H_i\).
Thresholds and floors:

\[ \begin{array}{c|c|c} \text{Level} & \text{Threshold } \tau & \text{Floor } F \\ \hline \text{warning} & 70 & 65 \\ \text{critical} & 80 & 75 \\ \text{emergency} & 90 & 85 \end{array} \] \[ S_{\text{override}} = \begin{cases} \max(S_{\text{raw}}, F_{\text{emerg}}) & S_{\max} \ge 90 \\ \max(S_{\text{raw}}, F_{\text{crit}}) & \text{else if } S_{\max} \ge 80 \\ \max(S_{\text{raw}}, F_{\text{warn}}) & \text{else if } S_{\max} \ge 70 \\ S_{\text{raw}} & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \]

Emergency level sets hardOverride = true. Set \(S_{\text{instant}} = S_{\text{override}}\).

4.6 Step 6: Composite Smoothing (EMA) with Emergency Reset

If hardOverride is true, \(S_{\text{final}} = S_{\text{instant}}\) and the composite EMA state is not updated (bypass).

Otherwise, apply time‑aware EMA to the composite score:

\[ \alpha_{\text{comp}}(t) = \begin{cases} 1 - \exp\!\left(-\dfrac{\Delta t}{\tau_{\text{comp}}}\right) & \tau_{\text{comp}} \text{ provided and } \Delta t > 0 \\ \alpha_{\text{comp}}^{\text{fixed}} & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \] \[ S_{\text{final}}(t) = \alpha_{\text{comp}}(t) \cdot S_{\text{instant}} + \bigl(1 - \alpha_{\text{comp}}(t)\bigr) \cdot \bar S_{\text{comp}}(t-1) \]

Emergency reset rule: When the override level transitions from emergency to any lower level, the composite EMA state is immediately reset: \(\bar S_{\text{comp}}(t) = S_{\text{instant}}\) and its timestamp is set to the current time. This prevents the EMA from artificially keeping the score elevated after the emergency has cleared.

4.7 Step 7: Explainability – Contribution Percentages

For a given set of scores \(X_i\) (either smoothed or raw), the weighted contribution uses the same effective confidence as fusion:

\[ C_i = X_i \cdot w_i \cdot c_i^{\text{eff}}, \quad c_i^{\text{eff}} = \max(c_i^{\text{raw}}, \text{softFloor}) \]

Total \(C_{\text{total}} = \sum_i C_i\). The percentage contribution is:

\[ P_i = \frac{C_i}{C_{\text{total}}} \times 100\% \]

Percentages are rounded in descending order of \(C_i\) (largest first) to ensure \(\sum P_i = 100\%\) exactly, with the last module receiving the remainder. The top two drivers (largest \(P_i\)) are reported for both smoothed and instant scores.

5. Alert Level Mapping

Based on final composite score \(S_{\text{final}}\):

\[ \text{AlertLevel} = \begin{cases} \text{EMERGENCY} & S_{\text{final}} \ge 90 \\ \text{CRITICAL} & 80 \le S_{\text{final}} < 90 \\ \text{HIGH} & 70 \le S_{\text{final}} < 80 \\ \text{MEDIUM} & 50 \le S_{\text{final}} < 70 \\ \text{LOW} & 30 \le S_{\text{final}} < 50 \\ \text{NOMINAL} & S_{\text{final}} < 30 \end{cases} \]

8. Summary of the Complete Inference Pipeline

  1. Normalisation – Compute \(S_i^{\text{raw}}\) for each module using domain formulas (Layer 1).
  2. Raw Confidence – Compute \(c_i^{\text{raw}}\) from recency, health, visibility, model confidence.
  3. Per‑Module EMA – Obtain \(S_i^{\text{smooth}}\) using time‑aware \(\alpha_i(t)\).
  4. Confidence‑Weighted Fusion – Compute \(S_{\text{raw}}\) with effective confidences \(c_i^{\text{eff}} = \max(c_i^{\text{raw}}, \text{softFloor})\). Exclude modules below minModuleConfidence.
  5. Multi‑Level Override – Determine \(S_{\text{override}}\) based on \(\max(S_i^{\text{raw}}, S_i^{\text{smooth}})\). Emergency sets hardOverride = true.
  6. Composite EMA – Smooth \(S_{\text{override}}\) to \(S_{\text{final}}\) (unless emergency hard override). Handle emergency exit reset.
  7. Explainability – Calculate contribution percentages using \(c_i^{\text{eff}}\) (same as fusion). Report top two drivers.
  8. Alert Level – Map \(S_{\text{final}}\) to a discrete level.
